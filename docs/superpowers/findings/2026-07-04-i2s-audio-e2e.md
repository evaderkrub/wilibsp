# I2S full-duplex audio — on-device E2E findings (2026-07-04)

Validated on the FreeWili2 (RP2350B **rev 3**) bench unit over the CMSIS-DAP probe.
Firmware: `apps/hello_audio` at master `37c0660` (increment 1 merge). fs = 16 kHz;
1 kHz tone out the codec while the codec ADC (3.5 mm mic) captures — full-duplex on
one I2S bus (PIO0 SM0), TX zero-CPU ring DMA + RX ping-pong DMA on a **shared**
`DMA_IRQ_0`.

## Result: PASS (electronic evidence) — full-duplex confirmed on silicon

Flashed + `** Verified OK **` over cmsis-dap. 24 s RTT capture (`fw rtt -s 24`)
covering boot + ~2.5 SILENCE→SPEAKER→JACK cycles. All electronic gates pass; the two
acoustic/visual items are user-confirmed (see below).

### 1. Codec alive and the ADC/mic path is enabled
- `codec: nau88c10 init done (16 kHz, speaker)`
- `codec: rev(0x3F)=0x01A pm2(0x02)=0x015` → `codec: input path ready`
  - rev `0x01A` ≠ 0/0xFFFF (live part); PM2 `0x015` = ADCEN+PGAEN+BSTEN (mic path armed).

### 2. Output routing switches at the register level (proves the switch hit silicon)
Read back on each `set_output()`:
- **SPEAKER:** `R3=0x0ED R36=0x03F R38=0x004 R45=0x005` — speaker vol full, HP off, 5 V boost on.
- **JACK:**    `R3=0x0ED R36=0x040 R38=0x001 R45=0x001` — speaker **muted** (bit6), HP **on**.

Matches the spec's empirically-derived routing exactly.

### 3. Full-duplex RX capture never starves (the key integration gate)
The `vu:` peak stream is continuous across all states; the block counter climbs
**monotonically 1 → 1489 over 24 s = ~62 blocks/s = ~16 ms/block**, i.e. exactly
256 frames at 16 kHz with no gaps. This proves:
- RX capture runs concurrently with TX playback (true full-duplex), and
- **the shared `DMA_IRQ_0` change works on silicon**: the audio-capture handler and
  the ST7796 flush handler coexist — the display keeps drawing (state banners + the
  VU-bar `fill_rect`s execute) while capture never drops a block. This was the #1
  risk of the whole increment (`irq_set_exclusive_handler` → `irq_add_shared_handler`
  in `bsp/audio/audio_capture.c`) and it is **confirmed healthy**.

### 4. Mic capture is live (RX slot alignment correct — no knob needed)
On-device peaks sit near the noise floor (~20–150 of 32767) with occasional real
acoustic spikes (e.g. blk 609–657: 3315, 2554, 1801, 1600; blk 1489: 875). Capture
tracks real sound rather than sitting stuck at 0 or railed at full-scale, so
`AUDIO_MIC_I2S_SLOT = 1` (right slot) and the `i2s_duplex.pio` sample phase are
correct as harvested — the documented RX-alignment bring-up knob was **not** needed.

## Bug found + fixed on the bench: ~9 Hz "clicky jack" (MCLK/LRCK sample slip)

**Symptom:** the jack output had a steady periodic tick (speaker sounded clean).

**Ruled out** (systematic bisection): PIO/BCLK clock jitter (speaker plays the same
jittery stream and was clean); the un-muted routing-switch pop (tick was periodic, not
one-shot); the per-block LCD VU redraw (suppressing it changed nothing).

**Root cause (objectively confirmed):** high-resolution FFT of the *clean speaker*
1 kHz tone showed sidebands at **±9.2 Hz** (−17 dB) — a real modulation on the DAC
output itself, present on both paths but masked on the speaker. 9.2 Hz is exactly
`MCLK/256 − LRCK`: MCLK is an **integer** PWM divide of `clk_sys`, so at 250 MHz it is
`250e6/61 = 4.0984 MHz` (not 4.096) → the codec's MCLK-direct DAC consumes at
`4.0984e6/256 = 16009 Hz`, while the data was framed at `LRCK = 16000 Hz`. The DAC
slipped ~9 samples/s → the tick. (A deliberately-wrong `MCLK = 4.032 MHz` test drove
the slip to ~249 Hz and destroyed the tone, confirming the DAC clocks off MCLK.)

**Fix** (`bsp/audio/audio_i2s_duplex.c`): lock the I2S frame rate to `MCLK/256` by
deriving the PIO clkdiv from the *same* integer MCLK divider (`div = ticks*8/3`)
instead of the nominal `sample_rate`. Now data-in == data-out → no slip. Net pitch is
`16009` vs `16000` Hz = **+0.06 %**, inaudible.

**Verification** (EMEET mic, jack→external speaker): the ±9.2 Hz sideband dropped from
**−17.1 dB → −44.1 dB** (~27 dB, into the noise floor) on both the speaker and jack
windows; ±18 Hz second-order gone (−55…−59 dB). User confirmed the jack "sounds great."
